Ancient India Kings & Rulers

Ashoka

Ashoka, also known as Ashoka the Great, was a Mauryan emperor who ruled over much of the Indian subcontinent from 268 to 232 BCE. He is remembered for his conversion to Buddhism and his efforts to promote peace and non-violence.

Chandragupta Maurya

Chandragupta Maurya was the founder of the Mauryan Empire, one of the largest empires in ancient India. He reigned from 321 to 297 BCE and is credited with unifying much of the subcontinent under his rule.

Harshavardhana

Harshavardhana, also known as Harsha, ruled over northern India from 606 to 647 CE. He was a patron of art, literature, and music, and his reign is often considered a golden age of Indian history.

Akbar

Akbar was the third Mughal emperor, ruling from 1556 to 1605 CE. He is known for his military conquests, religious tolerance, and administrative reforms. Akbar's reign is often seen as the zenith of the Mughal Empire.
